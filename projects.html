BeLine — Posture-Tracking Wearable + App

End-to-end product definition for a posture-tracking wearable, focusing on habit formation, behavioral feedback, and long-term engagement in a hardware-software ecosystem.

What I built
  • Defined the MVP scope across hardware, mobile app, and data flow.
  • Designed the primary user journey: setup → pairing → calibration → daily posture feedback.
  • Translated ambiguous user needs into clear functional requirements and edge cases.
  • Mapped system interactions between sensors, mobile app, and notification logic.
What I tracked
  • Activation rate from device pairing to first posture session.
  • Weekly active usage and streak-based habit retention.
  • Impact of notification timing and coaching nudges on behavior change.
  • Drop-offs across onboarding and calibration steps.

Key takeaway: Behavior-change products succeed when hardware constraints, onboarding clarity, and feedback loops are designed together and not separately.

Calypso — AI Travel Planning Concept

Product strategy and MVP definition for an AI-powered travel planning assistant, focused on reducing planning friction and decision fatigue.

What I built
  • Designed the MVP flow from user preferences to AI-generated itineraries.
  • Defined value proposition and differentiation versus manual planning tools.
  • Structured onboarding to balance personalization depth and speed.
  • Outlined system prompts and guardrails for itinerary generation.
What I tracked
  • Time-to-first itinerary as a measure of perceived speed and value.
  • Save, edit, and share actions as engagement signals.
  • Drop-offs across onboarding questions.
  • Repeat usage intent for future trips.

Key takeaway: AI products deliver value only when speed, clarity, and user control are more balanced since personalization isn’t always better.

Mochileros Sin Fronteras — Travel Website Concept

UX-focused travel website concept designed to encourage exploration, serendipity, and lightweight trip inspiration.

What I built
  • Defined site architecture and core discovery flows.
  • Designed multi-language and currency toggles for international users.
  • Created a “surprise destination” feature to encourage exploration.
  • Outlined filtering and search logic for flexible trip discovery.
What I tracked
  • Search, filter, and destination save behavior.
  • CTA clicks from inspiration to planning actions.
  • Engagement with the surprise destination feature.
  • Return visits as a proxy for inspiration value.

Key takeaway: Exploration-driven UX works best when users can switch seamlessly between surprise and control without increasing cognitive load.
